About ace-memory

♠ Ace Give every AI tool persistent memory.Free · Local · Gets smarter with use

🧠 PVM is more than AI memory. The same engine also powers causality tracing for law firms, anomaly detection for audits, contradiction discovery for investigations, and more. See full use cases →

The author is not a programmer. This MCP was hand-built in VS Code — I can't read code well, and honestly I'm not sure what kind of mess I've made. If you're a developer, the source is open: please audit it and email me bugs. I will fix them.

New versions are in active development. Follow the repo, or email me to guide the work.

Finally: please respect my copyright and license. Don't exploit this for free — the author comes from a business background and knows exactly how that game is played.

Every time you open an AI tool, it forgets who you are.

You tell ChatGPT your tech stack, your preferences, your project goals. The next day — gone. You tell Cursor your architecture decisions. Next session — blank. Every conversation starts from scratch.

The existing solutions solve this. But they do it by shipping your data to US clouds, locking you into subscriptions, and hiding how your memories evolve. You're paying to lose control.

That's backwards. Your memories should belong to you.

Ace is the memory layer that sits between you and any AI tool. It remembers everything — and gets smarter every time you use it.

It's not just storage. It's memory that learns.
